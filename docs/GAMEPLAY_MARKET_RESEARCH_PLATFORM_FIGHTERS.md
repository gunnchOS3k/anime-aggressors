# Gameplay Market Research — Platform Fighters

## Super Smash Bros.

- **Input grammar:** Direction + attack / direction + special; shield, dodge, grab on dedicated buttons.
- **Damage model:** Percentage-based; higher damage increases knockback on subsequent hits.
- **Knockback:** Scales with victim damage, move power, and weight; launch angles create recoveries and edge-guards.
- **Blast zones:** Leaving the stage boundary costs a stock; respawn with invulnerability.
- **Recovery:** Aerial drift, jumps, and specials create chase/recovery gameplay.
- **Rules:** Stock, time, stamina, and team variants.
- **Camera:** Frames all fighters with padding; zooms out when fighters separate; zooms in modestly when clustered; follows launches without losing stage context; hard hits use brief freeze and directional emphasis.
- **Impact:** Hitlag freezes attacker and victim; launch trails and KO zoom sell consequences.

## Rivals of Aether / Rivals 2

- Elemental identity affects combat readability and arena mood.
- Competitive platform-fighter pacing with readable 2D logic in 2.5D presentation.
- Strong character silhouettes and VFX tied to element.

## Brawlhalla

- Accessible inputs and broad platform support.
- Weapon/signature readability for casual players.
- Fast match cadence.

## Fantasy Strike

- Reduced input complexity; strategy through spacing and reads.
- Beginner-friendly without removing high-level play.

## Traditional Fighting Games

- Character select before match.
- Stage select and versus presentation.
- Round start signals (Ready / Fight).
- Winner presentation with rematch flow.

## Anime Aggressors Target

Anime Aggressors should use **simple platform-fighter controls** like Smash/Fantasy Strike, **elemental identity** like Rivals, and **anime-style spectacle** through aura, hitstop, camera impulse, trails, and launch.

Design pillars:

1. Easy to control, hard to master.
2. Visually exciting low-poly models with strong VFX.
3. Clear hit consequences (damage %, knockback, stocks).
4. Satisfying knockback and launch arcs.
5. Anime/superhero impact via hitlag, sparks, and camera punch.
