# Design: Accessibility and Depth

Anime Aggressors uses one **input grammar** for every fighter:

- Attack + direction → grounded/aerial attacks
- Special + direction → specials; Down + Special → super
- Shield, dodge, jump — same across roster

## Beginner path

- **2–3 interaction combos** per fighter (documented in Combo Guide)
- **Aura Charge**: Hold Shield + Special — builds meter without memorizing strings
- Beginner characters (e.g. Ember Vale) have forgiving frame data and clear teaching routes

## Intermediate path

- Spacing, aerial routes, energy clash tug-of-war
- Aura level 2+ modest special enhancement
- Stage control and blast-zone awareness

## Advanced path

- Aura level 3 → Super Ready release
- Beam clash stability from aura investment
- Character-specific super timing and cancel windows

## Balance principles

- Beginner characters are **not weak** — they have lower execution ceiling, not lower damage floor
- Advanced characters are **not overpowered** — higher skill ceiling, not free wins
- Aura charge has **risk**: slow movement, interruptible, decays on hit

## In-game hints

Training mode surfaces:

- Beginner / intermediate / advanced combo routes
- Aura Charge input and level thresholds
- Super Ready and Beam Clash prompts

Move List includes universal **Aura Charge** card with input, purpose, and risk.
